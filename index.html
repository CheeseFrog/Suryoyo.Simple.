 Nominative Pronouns & the Copula

> In Surayt, the copular verb 'to be' in its various states (is am are

  was were) assigns a subject by truncating the nominative pronouns

> Minus the duplication all there is to know are these highlights:
 7 nominative pronouns (6 + f3S), -yo, -wa/-way-, ko, gd

Nominative/Subjective Pronouns — in 3rd/2nd/1st person, Single, Plural:

 3S

hiye|hiya = he|she

 3P

hne = they

 2S

hat = you

 2P

hatu = youse

 1S

eno/ono = I

 1P

aħna = we

Present 'be' — just truncate pronoun (except 3S -yo):

 3S

-yo = (he/she) is

 3P

-ne = (they) are

 2S

-at = (you) are

 2P

-atu/utu = (youse) are

 1S

-no = (I) am

 1P

-na = (we) are

  eg. NOUNS: (Hne)^ yolufe-ne = They are students (3P)

> See Verbs III section for how to apply the subject (actor) to verbs

  eg. VERBS: (Ko)* malakh|malkho-no = I am walking (m|f 1S)

Past 'be' — just add the infix -way- (except 3S -wa):

 3S

-wa = (he/she) was

 3P

-wayne = (they) were

 2S

-wayt = (you) were

 2P

-waytu = (youse) were

 1S

-wayno = (I) was

 1P

-wayna = (we) were

  eg. NOUNS: (Ono)^ yolufto-wayno = I was a student (f1S)

  eg. VERBS: (Ko)* malkho-wayno = I was walking (f1S)

> Alternatively, -wa may simply follow the copular suffix
  eg. Rabe-way-ne = Rabe-ne-wa = They were big (3P)

^ Whilst redundant, one may emphasise the full pronoun prior

* Ko emphasises activity of present & past verbs akin to English '-ing'

  ie. without "Ko", "malakh-no" is just a query, "I am to walk?"

Future 'be' + verb conjugation — just add Gd/G- (will):

 Gd + (verb + 'be' present form) = {} will {}

  eg. gd malakh-no = I will walk (m 1S)

 Gd + (verb + 'be' past form) = {} would've {} (past conditional)

  eg. gd malakh-wayno = I would've walked (m 1S)

  eg. gd malkh-utu-wa/malkhu-way-tu = Youse would've walked (2P)

Conjugating pronouns with 'be' - is thus simple duplication:

 Present: hiyeyo|hiyayo, hatat, onono, hnene, hatutu, aħnana

 Past: hiyewa|hiyawa, hatwayt, onowayno, hnewayne, hatwaytu, aħnawayna

  ie. Hat-at = you are (2S)     cf. Hat-wayt = you were (2S)

  eg. Eno-no ou nuhro shariro = I am the light truthful (1S)

  eg. Hne-ne qadmoye = They are first (3P)

> The future tense of the verb 'be' (will be) is "gd how-"

 Future: (gd +): howe|howyo, howat, howeno, howen, howutu, howina

  eg. gd how-ina Yolufe = we will be students (1P)

 Objective & Possessive Pronouns

Objective pronouns — used to indicate person is the object (acted upon
by a subject) of prepositions & transitive verbs

 3S

-e|a = him|her

 3P

-aye = them

 2S

-okh|akh = you (m|f)

 2P

-aykhu = youse

 1S

-i = me

 1P

-an = us

  eg. Ko malakhno aʕm-aye = I am walking with them (3P)

  eg. Kitl-an-lokh kthowo men-a = We (1P) have a book from her (f3S)

> See Special Prepositions & Verbs III sections for definitions

Possesive pronouns — are made by prepositioning d- > did-* (of)

 3S

dide|a = his|hers

 3P

didaye/athe = theirs

 2S

didokh|akh = yours (m|f)

 2P

didaykhu/athkhu = yours

 1S

didi = mine

 1P

didan = ours

> Dropping the did- for -eyd- (or altogether) forms a possive suffix

  eg. Eshmo dide = Eshm-eyd-e = Eshm-e = His name (m 3S)

* Classic Syriac employs dil- in place of did- (ie. Didi = Dili)

 Demonstrative Pronouns

Demonstrative Pronouns (by proximity, Single, Plural)

S

hano|hathe = this (m|f)

P

hani = these

S

hawo|hayo = that (m|f)

P

hanek = those

  eg. Hani-ne a Suryoy-eyd-an = These-are the Suryoyos-ours (1P)

  eg. Bi dukth-ayo qadishto = In that place holy (f)

> Dropping the "h" produces the suffix form (bolded part)

  eg. Ou lalyo hano = Ou laly-ano = Ad-lalyo* = This night

  eg. Man-yo hathe? = Man-y-athe? = Who is this (f)?

* The prefix "Ad" can also mean this agendered (eg. Ad-yowmo = This day)

 Articles: Definite (the) & Indefinite (a)

Definite article (the) has gender & plurality, & precedes proper names:

 ou|ie = the (m|f)

  eg. Ou Steyfo ħawr-eydi-yo = The Steve friend-mine-is

  eg. Ie malkutho ruħonayto = The kingdom spiritual

 a|an = the (plural | plural when subject begins with vowel)

  eg. An abnay-dan = The sons-our = Our sons

Indefinite article (ie. 'a book') is implied but emphasised with:

 ħa|ħdo = a/one (m|f)

  eg. Kitli [ħdo] shoʕtho = Kitli shoʕtho = I have [an] hour

 Special Prepositions: from, to, in, of, by

> Prepositions that can be prefixed to articles (see Articles section)

 Men (cl) / Me / M- = from         >   Mou|Mi|Ma = from the (m|f|P)

 Lwoth (cl) / L- = to              >   Lou|Li|La = to the (m|f|P)

  eg. Azan mou athro = We went from the land (m)

      l-duktho ħreto = To a place another (f)

  eg. Mou yamino lou semolo = From the right to the left

 Bġaw (cl) / B- = in              >   Bou|Bi|Ba = in the (m|f|P)

 D- = of                           >   Dou|Di|Da = of the (m|f|P)

  eg. Bi dukth-ayo d-babon-eyd-an = In that (f) place of our fathers

  eg. Ou qolo dou Shemʕun basimo-yo = The voice of the Simon is nice

 Se / S- = by/beside              >   Sou|Si|Sa = by/beside the (m|f|P)

  eg. Azi-no sou dukano di Maryam = I went by the shop of (the) Maryam

  eg. A qlithe sa qanye-ne = The keys beside the pens-are

> These prefixes are first modified when attaching objective pronouns*

M > Men-

L > El-

B > Eb-

D > Did-*

S > Sid-

  eg. Men-okh L-el-an = From you to (to) us

  eg. Kit flefle eb-aye = There's chilli in them (idiom: 'hyperactive')

  eg. Athi-li egartho men-aykhu = Came to me a letter from youse

* See Objective & possessive pronouns section

 Existence is, Possession has, & Ability can

Kit/Kito   (cl. Ko-Ith) = there is / there exists

Layt/Layto (cl. Lo-ith) = there isn't / there doesn't exist

Existence: is & isn't / are & aren't / was & wasn't / were & weren't

  eg. Kit aloho = There is a god (idiom: 'divine jugment will come')

  eg. Layt mede ħatho = There isn't anything new

> Conjugate with subjective pronouns ('to be') to assign person & tense

  eg. Kit-wa slutho athmel = There was (3S) a prayer yesterday

  eg. Lat-atu noquse = Youse aren't (2P) lacking

  eg. Lat-yo shrolo = It/he/she (3S) isn't true

Possession: have & haven't / had & hadn't

> Conjugate with -l- (to) & objective pronoun^ to indicate possession

 Kitl- = Have

 Latl- = Haven't

  eg. Kitl-okh yuqr-utho = You have (2S) weight (respect)

  eg. Latl-i lebo = I haven't (1S) heart (idiom: 'not in the mood')

> Past possession is indicated by terminal -wa*

  eg. Kitl-an-wa ħago = We had (1P) a party

  eg. Latl-a-wa qlitho = She hadn't (f3S) a key

Ability: can & can't / could've & couldn't have

> Conjugate with -b- (in) & objective pronoun^ to indicate ability

 Kib-  = Can

 Layb- = Cannot

  eg. Kib-i othe-no = I can come | Can I come? (1S)

  eg. Layb-akh hozat-li = You can't (f2S) see me

> Past ability is indicated by terminal -wa*

  eg. Kib-okh-wa malkh-at = You could've walked (2S)

  eg. Layb-an-wa saymi-na mede = We couldn't have done anything (1P)

^ See Special Propositions & Verbs III Further conjugation sections

  Exception for 3P/2P: -aye & -ayku is shortened for -e & -khu,
 with -b- pronounced as p, whilst -l- is made silent

  ie. Kip-e/Lap-e (they can/n't)     Kip-khu/Lap-khu (youse can/n't)

      Kit-e/Lat-e (they have/n't)    Kit-khu/Lat-khu (youse have/n't)

* Alternative form exists with the infix -way-

  eg. Kit-lan-wa = Kit-way-lan = We had (1P)

 Gender & Plurality

Noun & adjective gender
> A noun ending in -o without t/th prior is typically* male.
 The female is formed (if a noun can be both) with -to/-itho^.

  eg. Malfono (m) | Malfonitho (f) = Teacher

  eg. Zʕuro | Zʕirto = Child (m|f)

Noun & adjective plurality
> The -o becomes -e/-one for male plural*. Female plural (if valid)
 replaces -to/-itho for -othe/-yothe (-otho/-yotho alternatively).

  eg. Malfone (m P) | Malfonyothe (f P) = Teachers

  eg. Zʕure = Children

> Adjectives agree with the gender & pluratity of their subject

  eg. Bartho shafirto-yo = Girl beautiful (f) she is

  eg. Bote* shafire lat-li = Houses beautiful (m P) I haven't

  eg. Lebo basimo kit-li = Heart pleasant (m) have I

> Verbs also agree, see Verbs section for details on conjugation

  eg. Hiye ko malekh | Hiya ko malkho = He|She is walking (m|f)

  eg. Ono ko rohatno|ruhtono = I am runnning (m|f)

  eg. Rahet|Rahito lou bayto = he|she ran home (m|f)

* Some male noun roots have a terminal t & are made plural irregularly
 eg. Bayto (house) > Bote (houses) cf. Baytone

^ Similarly, some female noun roots end in an o (eg. Emo = mother)

 Verbs

 I. Roots & Classes of Verbs

> Verbs are conjugated from 3-4 letter roots into various forms:

  Root > Class > Mood > Active/Passive > Person > Tense

  eg. g-r-sh (pull) > grsh-le (he pulled) > grshle-wa (he had pulled)…

> Often, roots can also found in related nouns or adjectives

  eg. Sh-a-l: Shuwolo (a question) > Mshayel (ask)

  eg. k-r-i: Karyo (short) > Makri (shorten)

  eg. R-ħ-m: Raħme (mercy) > Marħm (pity)

> Verbs exist in three broad classes that are conjugated differently#

 - Ground: A simple neutral action

  eg. Zbat (hold), Ħzay (see), Rhat (run), Zokh (go), Sm (do)

 - Intensive: An intense action; prefixed with M-*

  eg. Mhalaq (throw), Mshayel (ask), Mthakr (recall), Mrabi (nurture)

 - Causative: An action to cause change; prefixed with Ma-^

  eg. Mafhm (clarify), Madʕr (return), Makhli (empty), Makri (shorten)

> Roots can exist across different verbal classes

  eg. D-m-kh: Dmakh (G: sleep) > Madmkh (C: put to rest)

  eg. F-h-m: Fham (G: understand) > Mafhm (C: clarify)

* Not to be confused with the preposition M- (from)

^ This is akin to many verbs that end in -ify (to change) in English

# The details of where/how vowels are placed is beyond this scope

 II. Verbal Moods & Tense

> Prior examples are all in agendered imperative mood (command)

> Each verb also has a basic infinitive mood*, which is gendered
 & exists in the present or future context, usually preceded by:

  Ko (doing) or Gd (will do), or a conjunction

  eg. Ko goresh = He is pulling

  eg. Gd gursho = She will be pulling

  eg. Itha domakh|dmkho = If he|she sleeps

* Which occasionally may be the same as the imperative (eg. mafhm)

> Verbal noun mood follows the rules of a typical noun & may follow
 definite articles. If causative, the prefix Ma- becomes Te-.

  eg. Ou grosho (the pull) / dmokho (the sleep) / Zboto (the capture)

  eg. Ou te-dʕoro dou malko = The returning of the king

 III. Further Conjugation

> Prior examples are all active voice verbs in 3rd person singular

> Passive voice verbs indicate being acted upon rather than doing.
 These are prefixed with Mi-, or Mita- for causative class verbs:
  eg. Mhalaq = throw           >   Mi-halaq = being thrown
 eg. Madʕr = return           >   Mita-dʕr = being returned

> The subject of a verb acts, and the object is acted upon.

  English uses a SVO (subject-vowel-object) order (eg. She loved him).

  Sureth verbal conjugation uses VSO order (ie. = Rħim-la-le).

> This applies to transitive verbs that perform actions to an object.
 Intransitive verbs, meaning no object is being acted upon, thus only
 employ the subjective pronoun in VS order (eg. Rahet-no = I ran)

> The subject (actor) is usually* assigned with the subjective pronouns:

 3S

-|o = he|she {}

 3P

-i = they {}

 2S

-at = you {}

 2P

-utu = youse {}

 1S

-no|ono = I {} (m|f)

 1P

-ina = we {}

  eg. Damikh-utu (youse slept), Rahit-at (you ran), Ko Mhalaq-no (I am
     thowing), Yaqith-i (they burnt), Ko ruħm-ono (I love)

> The object acted upon is assigned with -l- (to) + objective pronouns:

 3S

-le|la = {} him|her

 3P

-e/le^ = {} them

 2S

-lokh|lakh = {} you (m|f)

 2P

-khu/lkhu* = {} youse

 1S

-li = {} me

 1P

-lan = {} us

  ie. Mħe-li hie = Mħe-li-le = I hit him

  ie. Hze-li el-i radayto = Hze-li-li radayto = I found myself a car

  eg. Ko mhalaq-no-le muklo = I'm throwing to him food

  eg. Gd Msaʕd-utu-way-la = Youse would've help her

* As in the example Rħim-la-le however, note the use of an 'objective
 pronoun' -la- as the subject (she). This exception applies to active
 transitive verbs of indicative tense (past simple/perfect).

  eg. Mhalaq-lan-wa ou zabno = We had thrown the time

  eg. Grsh-la-li-wa me idi = She pulled me from my hand

  eg. Hze-lkhu ou bahro = Youse had seen the light

  eg. Shmʕ-e^ qolo = They heard a voice

^ Following a consonant, the -l- isn't pronounced in 3P/2P
